Configuration Checking

Ensure all system parameters are properly configured before launch.

Fuel Pressure - Must be between 2.0-3.5 MPa

Engine Temperature - Optimal range: -50°C to 500°C

Weather Conditions - Wind speed must be < 30 m/s

Payload Weight - Must not exceed max capacity

Rocket Design Parameters

Review key design parameters like thrust, stability, and weight distribution.

Thrust-to-Weight Ratio (TWR): Determines if the rocket can lift off. TWR > 1 is necessary.


Structural Integrity: The rocket must withstand forces like vibration, aerodynamic pressure, and mechanical stress.


Center of Gravity (CG) & Center of Pressure (CP): A stable rocket has its CG above CP to prevent flipping.


Engine Efficiency & Reliability: Factors include thrust, fuel efficiency, and cooling systems.


Aerodynamics: Nose cone shape, fin design, and drag coefficient affect flight stability.


Fuel Type & Efficiency: Liquid vs. solid fuel impacts burn rate, energy output, and controllability.


Payload Capacity: The weight of the satellite or cargo must be within the rocket’s carrying capacity.
